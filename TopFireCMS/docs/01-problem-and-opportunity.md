# Problem and Opportunity

## Current pain points

Agency teams often face the same issues across client projects:
- Content models differ wildly between projects.
- Publishing workflows are inconsistent or manual.
- Access control becomes hard to audit.
- Migrations across projects are slow and risky.
- Reporting on usage and reliability is fragmented.

## Why existing tools are not always enough

Off-the-shelf tools can be strong, but may not fit perfectly when:
- custom client workflows are frequent
- governance requirements are strict
- long-term cost and ownership are strategic concerns
- templates and automation need to mirror agency operations

## Opportunity statement

Build a single internal platform that:
- standardizes delivery for all clients
- preserves flexibility for content modeling
- improves reliability and compliance posture
- accelerates onboarding through reusable blueprints

## Strategic opportunity areas

1. Client onboarding acceleration
- Blueprint-based setup for common project types.
- Shared component and schema libraries.

2. Editorial quality and governance
- Mandatory validation and approval policies.
- Full revision history and auditability.

3. Engineering productivity
- Reusable schema packages and migration tooling.
- Stable APIs for frontend teams.

4. Operational control
- Central observability and incident workflows.
- Tenant-aware usage tracking and guardrails.

## Constraints and boundaries

- This is not a public multi-agency SaaS.
- Product scope should avoid overengineering for external self-service.
- Security and recoverability remain non-negotiable.
- MVP must deliver practical value quickly.

## Opportunity scorecard

FireContent has high potential if we execute on:
- stable core architecture
- rigorous schema governance
- excellent editor experience
- disciplined rollout and support model
